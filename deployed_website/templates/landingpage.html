RadarVision uses advanced radar and machine learning to accurately classify drones and non-drones in real-time, enhancing safety and security.

Our Mission

RadarVision addresses critical safety and security challenges by using advanced radar technology to differentiate drones from other aerial entities such as birds. This innovation ensures reliable and efficient monitoring of airspace to prevent unauthorized activities and enhance security.

Micro Doppler Classification Project

Advanced Protective Measures Against Small Aerial Threats

Abstract Model and Dataset

Our project leverages a continuous wave (CW) radar operating at X-band (10 GHz) to build a diversified dataset comprising 4,849 micro-Doppler signature images of six different small aerial targets. These images train a deep convolutional neural network (DCNN) based on transfer learning to achieve high classification accuracy.

Classes of Aerial Targets

  • Three-Long-Blade Rotor
  • Three-Short-Blade Rotor
  • Bird
  • Bird + Mini-Helicopter
  • Drone
  • RC Plane

Why This Matters?

As small UAVs become more common, the ability to automatically detect and classify these targets is essential for ensuring security and safety. Our approach not only demonstrates high accuracy but also provides a robust solution for real-time identification of multiple aerial targets operating simultaneously.
